===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **her people**  - The word "her" refers to Jerusalem who is described as if she were a woman. AT: "her inhabitants" or "the people of the city" (UDB) (See: [[:ur:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification|Personification]] and [[:ur:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person|First, Second or Third Person]])
  * **treasures**  - "wealth"
  * **to restore their life**  - AT: "to save their life" or "to restore their stength"
  * **Look, Yahweh, and consider me**  - Here Jerusalem begins to speak directly to Yahweh. (See: [[:ur:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification|Personification]])
  * **Is it nothing to you, all you who pass by?**  - This is an accusation of the people who walk past Jerusalem and do not care for it. It has the form of a rhetorical question to sharply rebuke them. AT: "All you who pass by should care more for my affliction!" (See: [[:ur:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion|Rhetorical Question]])
  * **Is it nothing to you**  - Here Jerusalem continues to speak, but now to people who pass by. (See: [[:ur:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification|Personification]])
  * **Look and see**  - These words mean basically the same thing and are combined for emphasis. (See: [[:ur:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet|Doublet]])
  * **the sorrow that is being inflicted on me**  - AT: "the sorrow that Yahweh is inflicting upon me" (See: [[:ur:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive|Active or Passive]])
  * **on the day of his fierce anger**  - AT: "when he was fiercely angry" (See: [[:ur:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom|Idiom]])
